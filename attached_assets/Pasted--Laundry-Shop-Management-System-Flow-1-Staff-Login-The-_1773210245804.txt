
# Laundry Shop Management System Flow

## 1. Staff Login

The staff logs into the laundry shop system to access the dashboard and manage laundry orders. This ensures that only authorized users can use the system.

⬇️

## 2. Dashboard

After logging in, the staff will see the **Dashboard**, which shows a quick overview of the laundry shop operations such as:

* Total Orders
* Pending Orders
* Ready for Pickup

This helps the staff monitor the current status of all laundry transactions.

⬇️

## 3. Add Customer / Create Order

The staff clicks **Add Customer** to enter the customer's laundry order.

The system records:

* Customer Name
* Address
* Contact Number
* Email Address
* Laundry Service (Wash & Hang, Dry Cleaning, etc.)
* Weight of Clothes (kg)
* Total Price

The **system automatically records the current date** for the order.

⬇️

## 4. Order Appears in Pending List

Once the order is submitted, it will automatically appear in the **Pending Orders** section.

In this section:

* The staff can see the **Order ID, Customer Name, Date, and Status**.
* Clicking the **Customer Name** will show the full customer and order details.

⬇️

## 5. Laundry Processing

The staff processes the order step-by-step by **clicking buttons to update the order status**.

Order Status Flow:

Pending
⬇️
Washed
⬇️
Ready for Pickup
⬇️
Completed

Each step requires the staff to **click a button to move the order to the next stage**.

⬇️

## 6. Ready for Pickup

When the laundry is finished, the order will appear in the **Ready for Pickup** section. This means the clothes are ready for the customer to collect.

⬇️

## 7. Order Completed

Once the customer picks up the laundry, the staff marks the order as **Completed**.

⬇️

## 8. Order Saved in History

All completed orders are stored in the **History section**.

The history page shows:

* Customer Name
* Order ID
* Date
* Status (Completed)

Yes, your system **can automatically send an email to the customer when a status button is clicked**. This is actually a very good feature because it **keeps the customer updated about their laundry order**. 📧

Here is a simple explanation you can include in your **system features or documentation**.

---

# Automatic Email Notification Feature

## Function

The system will **automatically send an email notification to the customer whenever the order status changes**. This happens when the staff **clicks a status button** in the system.

---

# How It Works (System Flow)

1️⃣ Staff updates the order status by clicking a button.
Example:

* Accept Order
* Mark as Washed
* Mark as Ready for Pickup
* Mark as Completed

2️⃣ The system detects the **status change**.

3️⃣ The system automatically **sends an email notification to the customer's email address**.

4️⃣ The customer receives an email informing them about the **current status of their laundry order**.

---
